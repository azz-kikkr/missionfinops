Consultancies, MSPs, fractional CTOs

Kulshan for consultancies and fractional CTOs.

For the right consulting or MSP fit, MissionFinOps can scope a license arrangement that lets your team run Kulshan inside client engagements as a repeatable AWS assessment step.

Pricing is scoped after we talk about your team size, the kinds of engagements you run, and the volume of AWS accounts in scope. There is no public price list and no marketplace listing.

The repeatable week of work.

Most cloud consulting engagements start with the same week of manual checks: cost attribution, security baseline, DR posture, drift detection, tag governance, quota headroom, network topology. The work is necessary. It is rarely visible to the founder paying the invoice. And it does not really change shape from one engagement to the next.

Kulshan runs that week of work as one CLI command and writes a report. The output shape is the same across engagements, which means client conversations get more comparable over time, your associates are not re-inventing a checklist on every kickoff, and the parts that vary by client (their specific resources, their specific findings) are the parts your team should actually be reasoning about.

What a license arrangement may include.

Specifics depend on the scope you and MissionFinOps agree to. Possible elements:

engagement use
The right to run Kulshan inside the client engagements your practice operates, with the client's read-only AWS credentials.
repeatable workflow
A consistent AWS assessment workflow with the same output shape across multiple AWS accounts, regions, and clients.
support
Optional async support channel for engagement questions, with a response window agreed at scope time. Available subject to scope.
advance access
Optional advance access to upcoming Kulshan packs, subject to availability and scope. New packs ship when they ship; this is not a roadmap commitment.

What this is not.

Worth saying out loud before a procurement conversation.

This arrangement is operational. It is not any of the following:

  • Not a white-label license. The product stays Kulshan.
  • Not a redistribution license for the CLI itself. The license covers using Kulshan during your engagements, not repackaging or re-selling the tool to your clients.
  • Not a SaaS or multi-tenant deployment. Each consultant runs Kulshan from their own machine against the client's account with the client's read-only credentials.
  • Not a roadmap commitment. New packs ship when they ship. Advance access (when offered) is to whatever is being built, not a guaranteed feature list.
  • Not an AWS reseller relationship. MissionFinOps does not resell AWS, manage the client's AWS bill, or hold the client's AWS credentials.

Who this is for.

Good fit

  • Cloud consultancies running AWS engagements as their primary surface.
  • Fractional CTOs with multiple portfolio companies on AWS.
  • Independent FinOps practitioners running recurring assessments.
  • MSPs whose primary clients are AWS-heavy.

Not a fit

  • Big-4 / enterprise procurement (different process; ask if you want to explore).
  • GCP-first or Azure-first practices (Kulshan is AWS-only).
  • Anyone who needs to white-label as their own product.
  • Practices with very few active engagements; the value depends on repetition.

Pricing scoped after fit

There is no public price list. MissionFinOps scopes the arrangement after a short conversation about your team size, the kinds of engagements you run, and the volume of AWS accounts in scope.
